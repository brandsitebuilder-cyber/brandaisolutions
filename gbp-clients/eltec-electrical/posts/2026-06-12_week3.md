## Post 1: Don't Let Your Electric Blanket Become a Fire Hazard This Winter 🔥

Winter in the Cape means cold nights — and for many of us, an electric blanket is the go-to solution. But here's something most people don't think about: an electric blanket older than 5 years, or one that's been folded and stored in a cupboard all summer, can be a serious fire risk.

Before you plug yours in this season, do a quick check. Lay it flat, turn it on, and run your hand over it — any hot spots or uneven heating means the internal wiring is damaged. Never use a blanket with exposed wires, scorch marks, or a controller that gets unusually warm. And please — don't tuck it under the mattress and leave it on all night. That's how most electrical blanket fires start.

Stay warm, stay safe. If your plug points or circuits are tripping when you add winter appliances, give us a call — that's a sign your circuits are overloaded. ⚡

## Post 3: Rain, Wind, and Your Outside Electrics — Cape Winter Is Here 🌧️

If you live in the Northern Suburbs, you know this weather. Sustained rain, days of damp, and the kind of wind that finds every weak point in your outside electrical fittings.

Those outside plug points, the pool pump connection, the garden lights, the electric fence energiser — all of it takes a beating from June through August. Moisture gets into cracked covers and corroded connections, and suddenly your earth leakage starts tripping every time it rains.

A few things you can do right now: check that outside socket covers are properly sealed and not cracked. Make sure extension leads aren't lying in puddles. And if your earth leakage trips and won't reset — don't keep forcing it. That's your installation telling you something is wrong.
